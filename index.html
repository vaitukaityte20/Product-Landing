Care Tips

Extend the life, fragrance, and glow of your candle by following these expert care guidelines.

Trim the wick to 5–7 mm before every burn.
First burn: let wax melt edge-to-edge to prevent tunneling (≈1 hour per 2.5–3 cm of diameter).
Always burn on a heat-safe, level surface away from drafts.
Never leave a burning candle unattended; keep away from kids and pets.
Limit each session to 3–4 hours; let the candle cool 2 hours before relighting.
Keep the melt pool clean—no matches, wick trimmings, or liquids.
If the wick leans, gently recenter it while the wax is soft.
Stop using with about 1 cm of wax remaining.
Store cool and out of sun. A natural beeswax “bloom” is normal—polish with a soft cloth or warm air.

FAQ

Got questions? Here are answers to some of the most common things our customers ask.

Are Medūkis candles 100% beeswax?

Yes — pure beeswax and cotton wick, no paraffin or synthetic additives.

Do they smell strong?

The scent is natural and subtle — gentle honey aroma, not perfumed.

Why is my candle frosting or developing a white film?

This natural “bloom” is unique to pure beeswax and a sign of quality. Gently buff with a soft cloth or warm air to restore its sheen.

Can I reuse the candle jars or wax remnants?

Yes! Wax remnants can be melted and repurposed. Jars can be cleaned and reused — we recommend warm water and mild soap.

How long do they burn?

Approximate burn: Klasika Didžioji ~12 h, Kūgis ~11 h, Eglė ~6 h, hearts ~2–2.5 h each.

Where are these candles made?

Hand-poured in Lithuania by Medūkis (D. Vaitukaitienės eco apiary products).

Are your candles safe for people with allergies?

Our candles are unscented and free of synthetic additives. Most allergy-sensitive individuals find them safe, but consult a doctor if unsure.

Do you ship internationally?

Yes, we offer international shipping. Rates and delivery times vary by region — check our Shipping Policy for details.
